# prompts/user_meta_prompt.md


Write ONE meta-insight for this slide (40-50 words).


## YOUR TASK


You have been provided with:
1. APPROVED STYLE EXAMPLES (above)
2. SLIDE DATA (above) - category metrics showing YoY % changes and variance from forecast
3. NARRATIVE GUIDANCE (above, if any) - suggested framing based on data patterns
4. PREVIOUS INSIGHTS (above, if any)


Write an executive-ready insight that is analytically honest, constructive where warranted, and explains the primary drivers.


## PRIORITY ORDER (follow in this sequence)


1. Analytical honesty and correct interpretation
2. Slide-level synthesis (one coherent story)
3. Follow the NARRATIVE GUIDANCE framing if provided
4. Explain primary drivers (demand/units vs pricing/ASP)
5. Variety/rotation - vary structure from previous insights


If constraints conflict, prioritize synthesis + honesty over adding extra figures or phrases.


## FRAMING SELECTION (follow the NARRATIVE GUIDANCE if provided)


If a NARRATIVE GUIDANCE section appears above, use that framing. The framing is selected based on what the data naturally supports. Otherwise, choose the framing that best fits the data:

- **ACCURACY-LED** (when variance is small): Lead with accuracy achievement; drivers support
- **DRIVER-LED** (when ASP/unit dynamics tell the story): Lead with driver dynamics; accuracy as outcome
- **CONTRAST-LED** (when tight vs wide): Lead with contrast between performers
- **CONTEXT-LED** (when significant variance present): Attribute to external/market conditions; anchor against a stable category. Never lead with the miss itself.

Each framing is valid - use what fits the data. Do not force accuracy-led framing when the data has a more compelling driver or contrast story.

Do not enumerate categories sequentially. Spotlight at most two categories (or a range).


## SENTENCE STRUCTURE OPTIONS (vary across slides based on framing)


For **ACCURACY-LED**, rotate these structures:
- "[Category] delivered [accuracy phrase] at [X]pp, [driver clause]."
- "[Driver clause] kept [category] within [X]pp of forecast."
- "With [driver], [category] achieved [accuracy phrase] at [X]pp."
- "Forecasts tracked tightly as [category] landed within [X]pp, [driver]."

For **DRIVER-LED** (lead with dynamics, accuracy as outcome):
- "[Driver dynamic] played out across categories, with [category] landing within [X]pp."
- "Pricing strength cushioned softer demand, keeping [category] within [X]pp of forecast."
- "Inverse value-volume dynamics shaped results, with [category] achieving [X]pp accuracy."
- "[Driver] movements drove the story, with forecasts tracking within [X]pp."

For **CONTRAST-LED** (highlight the contrast):
- "{{category_a}} achieved [X]pp accuracy, while {{category_b}} diverged by [Y]pp on [driver]."
- "In contrast to {{category_a}}'s tight accuracy, {{category_b}} faced [Y]pp variance amid [cause]."
- "{{category_a}} tracked closely at [X]pp; conversely, {{category_b}} saw [Y]pp gap due to [cause]."
- "Mixed results emerged: {{category_a}} at [X]pp versus {{category_b}}'s [Y]pp divergence."

For **CONTEXT-LED** (contextualise variance, anchor against stability):
- "Market conditions shifted for {{category_a}}, driving a [Y]pp gap; {{category_b}} maintained alignment at [X]pp."
- "[External factor] contributed to {{category_a}}'s [Y]pp variance — {{category_b}} held within [X]pp in the same environment."
- "Conditions moved faster than expected for {{category_a}} at [Y]pp; {{category_b}} tracked closely at [X]pp."
- "{{category_a}} faced [Y]pp variance amid [market context]; broader results remained more directionally aligned."


## METHODOLOGY-VALIDATING LANGUAGE (use naturally when supported)


When the data supports it, you may include measured phrases that reinforce forecasting context:
- "captured underlying pricing dynamics"
- "directionality tracked to plan"
- "relative category performance aligned with expectations"
- For misses: "market magnitude shifted versus assumptions" / "conditions moved faster than expected"


Use naturally as part of the narrative - don't force it. Maximum one such phrase per insight.


## HONEST VARIANCE LANGUAGE (use when appropriate)


When variance needs discussion, be analytically honest:
- "diverged by X percentage points driven by [driver]"
- "faced X percentage points variance as [explanation]"
- "came in X percentage points below forecast amid [conditions]"
- "experienced headwinds at X percentage points below projections"

For CONTEXT-LED framing, embed variance in market/external context — do not lead with the miss. For other framings, use as supporting context only.


## CONSTRUCTIVE ACCURACY LANGUAGE (use when warranted)


When accuracy is genuinely tight, rotate phrases for variety.
- For very tight accuracy (<2 percentage points), "precision" is acceptable.
- Otherwise rotate: "achieved alignment", "tracked closely", "remained closely aligned", "delivered tight accuracy", "delivered on-point performance"


## HIGH-LEVEL SYNTHESIS (tell one coherent story)


Synthesize the slide into one narrative - connect the dots rather than listing every metric. Explain WHY (pricing dynamics, demand shifts, market conditions).


Bad: "Category A was 1.2 percentage points, Category B was 3.4 percentage points, Category C was 5.6 percentage points"
Good (accuracy-led): "Forecasts tracked tightly across categories, with pricing strength cushioning softer demand"
Good (driver-led): "Pricing strength cushioned softer demand across categories, keeping forecasts within tight tolerances"
Good (contrast-led): "Cookware delivered precision at 1.2 percentage points, while tabletop faced 10.6 percentage points variance amid demand softness"


## VARIETY REQUIREMENT (important for deck readability)


Check PREVIOUS INSIGHTS and vary:
- Framing type (accuracy-led, driver-led, contrast-led) - let data guide but avoid repetition
- Lead metric (dollars/revenue, units/demand, or ASP)
- Sentence structure and opener
- Vocabulary (rotate synonyms for common phrases)


If "ASP offset units" language appeared recently, use alternatives:
- "pricing strength cushioned volume softness"
- "price realization mitigated demand pressure"
- Or focus on the dominant driver only

Variety creates a more engaging deck - formulaic repetition should be avoided.


## CONSTRAINTS


- 35-45 words maximum
- Spell out "percentage points" (never abbreviate)
- Categories lowercase mid-sentence
- NO superlatives: "perfect", "exceptional", "outstanding"
- NO forward-looking speculation
- Maximum 2-3 percentage figures (not every metric for every category)


Return only the insight text.
